Put simply, my design process has 3 phases. A research phase, a solutioning phase and a high fidelity phase.
‍
The research phase consists of receiving work, whether it’s in the form of a discussion or a ticket. Followed by finding key users to interview, and finally getting familiar with their current process / needs.
‍
Solutioning is the phase that tends to take the longest. Mainly due to availability of the team or the complexity of the task. Solutioning consists of myself preparing the research into a digestible medium (workflows, diagrams etc.) so that the members of the meeting can get up to speed quickly and offer good ideas. Following that i present the analysis at the meeting and visualize the discussions on the fly into low fidelity mock-ups. I continue this loop until a final solution has been reached.
‍
I talk more about solutioning here in my write up on inspiring innovation. Something i’m highly passionate about and feel it is a trait i bring to any team i’m apart of.
‍
The last stage is to take the low fidelity solution and finalize it by creating a high fidelity prototype and prepare it for developer hand off.
